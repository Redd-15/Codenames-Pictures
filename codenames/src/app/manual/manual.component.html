Narrative

What are these strange symbols on the map? Codes for locations where spies must contact secret agents!

Two rival spymasters know the agent in each location. They deliver coded messages telling their field operatives where to go for clandestine meetings. Operatives must be clever. A decoding mistake could lead to an unpleasant encounter with an enemy agent - or worse, with the assassin! Both teams race to contact all their agents, but only one team can win.
Rooms

In the menu, pick the Create room option. Specify your username (3-20 characters) for the game and click Create. In the subsequent waiting room window, the room ID will appear. Share this ID with whoever you want to play with.

If you received an active room ID from a friend, pick the Join room option. Type the room ID and your username and click Join. While waiting for others to join the room, you will be prompted to pick a team and a role.

Theoretically, up to 9999 players can join the same room, but for a seamless experience, player count per room should not exceed 100 players.
Setup

Once in a room, split up into two teams - red and blue - of similar size and skill. You need at least four players (two teams of two) for a standard game.

Each team chooses one or more players to be their spymaster. The other players are field operatives.

Once all players are ready, you can start the game.

You can verify your selected username, role and team colour any time during the game on the very top of your screen. You can also check out all players in the room, along with their role and status by hovering over the Players button in the top right corner. Inactive players are not currently connected but may return to the game.
Board

Once in the game, operatives will see a grid of 4 x 5 symbols (5 x 4 on small screens). These represent locations where the field operatives can meet agents.

Spymasters will also see the same symbols with colour coding that shows who can be found in each location.
  • Blue corresponds to pictures that Blue Team must guess (locations with blue agents).
  • Red corresponds to pictures that Red Team must guess (locations with red agents).
  • Pale squares have innocent bystanders.
  • The black square hides an assassin.
The starting team has 8 pictures to guess, the other team has 7. The prompt on top of the grid will indicate whose turn it is to give a clue.
If you accidentally navigate away from the game window, keep your cool! Nothing has been lost yet. Just navigate back to the home page and select the option Rejoin previous room in the menu.
Beware! If you leave the game by clicking any of the Leave buttons, you will not be able to rejoin!
Game play

Spymasters take turns giving one-word clues. The word relates to one or more pictures in the grid. The field operatives try to guess which pictures their spymaster meant.

When a field operative touches a picture, a guess button appears. When this button is clicked, a guess is made, and it is revealed who is in that location. If it is one of their team's agents, the operatives may keep guessing locations related to that one-word clue. Otherwise, it is the other team's turn.

The first team to contact all their agents wins the game.
Taking turns
Teams take turns, beginning with the starting team. On your team's turn, a spymaster gives one clue, and the field operatives may make multiple guesses.
Giving a clue
If you are a spymaster, your job is to think up a clue that relates to some of the pictures your team is trying to guess. Your clue consists of one word that relates to those pictures and one number that tells how many of your pictures relate to that word. You are allowed to give a clue for only one picture but it's fun to try for two or more. Getting four pictures with one clue is a big accomplishment.

Making contact
When the spymaster gives a clue, his or her field operatives try to figure out what it means. The operatives indicate their official guess when one of them clicks the guess button on one of the pictures. The colour of the selected card is revealed.
  • If the field operative selects a picture belonging to his or her team, the operatives may continue making guesses (but they do not get another clue).
  • If the field operative touches the location with the assassin, this ends the game. The team that contacted the assassin loses.
  • Otherwise, the turn ends here.
Ending the turn
Your team's turn always has exactly one clue and one or more guesses. If the operatives' first guess is one of their team's pictures, they may make a second guess. If that's correct, they may make another guess, and so on. The turn ends
  • if they guess a picture that's not theirs,
  • if they choose to not guess anymore by pressing the End guessing button,
  • or if they have already made as many guesses as the number specified by the clue (the number of remaining guesses is displayed in the game prompt).
You might have received several clues for which you did not get all the pictures. You can guess these pictures instead of or in addition to those related to the current clue.
Game over

The game ends when one team has all their pictures covered. That team wins.

It is possible to win on the other team's turn if they guess your last picture. The game can end early if a field operative touches the location with the assassin. That operative's team loses.

On the game summary screen, the symbols of locations are displayed using colour coding corresponding to the agents found there. You can leave the room or start a new game.
Valid clues

Your clue should be one word, no spaces. It must be about the pictures, not about the patterns that cards make on the table or the letters in certain words.

You can allow your operatives to guess as many pictures as they want by selecting ? instead of a number. This is especially helpful if you have given clues for multiple pictures that your team has not guessed yet. The disadvantage is that the field operatives don't know how many pictures relate to the newest clue. The advantage is that they may guess as many pictures as they want.

You are allowed to use 0 as the number part of your clue. This means that none of your team's agents relate to that clue. If the spymaster gives 0 as the number, the usual limit on guesses does not apply.
